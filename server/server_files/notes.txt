India, officially the Republic of India (Bharat Ganrajya), is a diverse and populous country in South Asia, known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and rapidly growing economy. It is the world's largest democracy and the most populous country globally, with over 1.4 billion people.
Key Facts
Capital: New Delhi
Area: 3.287 million sq. km (seventh-largest in the world)
Government: Sovereign Socialist Democratic Republic with a Parliamentary system
Official Languages: Hindi and English, along with 20 other constitutionally recognized regional languages
Currency: Indian Rupee (INR)
Geography and Climate
India's geography is highly diverse, ranging from the snow-capped Himalayas in the north to the tropical rain forests and coastlines in the south. The country is a peninsula, bounded by the Bay of Bengal in the east, the Arabian Sea in the west, and the Indian Ocean to the south. The climate is broadly classified as a tropical monsoon one, with four official seasons.
Culture and History
India has one of the world's oldest and greatest civilizations, the Indus Valley Civilization dating back over 4,500 years. It is a land of profound unity in diversity, with multiple religions (Hinduism, Islam, Christianity, Sikhism, Buddhism, Jainism, etc.), ethnic groups, and cultural practices coexisting within a single nation. Traditional values, such as the importance of family and respect for elders, remain significant. India is also the birthplace of yoga, the number zero, the decimal system, and the game of chess.
Economy and Politics
Since gaining independence from British rule on August 15, 1947, India has made significant socio-economic progress. Its constitution came into force on January 26, 1950, establishing it as a republic. Today, India is a fast-growing major economy and a global hub for information technology services, with an expanding middle class. While poverty has declined sharply in recent decades, the country still faces challenges like economic inequality and environmental issues. The government operates as a federal parliamentary republic, with the President as the Head of State and the Prime Minister as the Head of Government.
